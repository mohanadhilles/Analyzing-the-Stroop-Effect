Their many theories that may explain the Stroop effect: The speed of Processing Theory: the interference occurs because the brain reads faster than it recognizes colors. Selective Attention Theory: interference occurs because recognizing colors requires more attention than reading words. Alternative tasks to try: Use emotional words such as "sad", "happy", "depressed" or "angry" mixed with neutral words such as "pen", "door" or "jeans", the words are colored and the participant is supposed to name the color. Research has revealed that individuals that are depressed are more likely to say the color of a negative word slower than the color of a neutral word
